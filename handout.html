AI Prompting Guides
Five ready-to-use prompts built around the challenges small business owners told us matter most
What this is
Five ready-to-use AI prompts built around the top challenges small business owners reported in our survey.
How to use it
Read the example first. Then use the fill-in-the-blank template — replace the highlighted blanks with details from your own business.
Where to paste it
Hit Copy on the template, paste it into Claude at claude.ai, fill in your details, and send. The more specific you are, the better the result.
One tip before you start
AI works best when you treat it like a smart new employee who knows nothing about your business yet. Give it context — it will give you better work.
The Prompting Framework
 — every prompt in this guide follows these four steps
1
Role & Goal
Tell AI who it is and what you need it to accomplish. This focuses it on your type of business and situation.
2
Steps
Break the task into clear numbered steps. This stops AI from taking shortcuts or missing something important.
3
Output
Describe exactly what you want back — format, length, tone. If you don't say, AI will guess.
4
Reflection
Ask AI to check its own work before giving it to you. A simple review step catches most mistakes early.
1
Marketing & Getting More Customers
Write a social media post — or a short series — that tells the right people about your business in a way they actually want to read.
1
Role & Goal
Tell AI what kind of business you run, where you post, and what you want customers to do — visit, order, call, follow.
2
Steps
Ask AI to choose a tone, write posts for your platforms, add a clear call to action, and suggest the best time to post.
3
Output
Specify how many posts, which platforms, and the tone — friendly, professional, exciting. Ask for hashtags if you want them.
4
Reflection
Ask AI to check that each post sounds natural, not like an ad, and that the call to action is clear and easy to follow.
Example prompt
You are a marketing assistant for a small Cantonese bakery in Flushing, Queens.

My goal is to get more local customers to visit and try our new seasonal special — a limited-edition almond pastry available this month only.

Step 1: Write 3 Instagram posts about the almond pastry. Make them feel warm and personal, not salesy.
Step 2: End each post with a simple call to action — "Visit us this weekend" or "Order online at [link]."
Step 3: Suggest 5 hashtags that local food lovers in Queens actually use.
Step 4: Tell me the best days and times to post for a neighborhood bakery.

Give me each post as a separate block, ready to copy and paste. Keep each post under 150 words.

Before you finish: read each post out loud in your head. If it sounds like an ad, rewrite it to sound like a real person who loves what they sell.
Fill-in-the-blank template  — replace highlighted blanks with your details
You are a marketing assistant for [your type of business] in [your neighborhood or city].

My goal is to get more customers to [visit / order online / call / follow us] by promoting [product or service you want to highlight].

Step 1: Write [number] posts for [Instagram / Facebook / both]. The tone should feel [warm / professional / fun / local].
Step 2: End each post with a clear call to action: [what you want people to do].
Step 3: Suggest [number] hashtags that customers in [your area] actually search for.
Step 4: Tell me the best days and times to post for a [type of business] in my area.

Give me each post as a separate block, ready to copy and paste. Keep each post under [100 / 150 / 200] words.

Before you finish: read each post as a customer would. If it sounds like an ad rather than a real recommendation, rewrite it.
💡
Give AI more context — it will give you better results
You can paste any of the following at the top of your prompt: your current bio or "About" section, examples of posts you like (or don't like), a description of your best customers, and what makes your business different from nearby competitors. The more AI knows about your business, the less generic the posts will feel.
2
Managing Finances & Cash Flow
Turn your numbers into a simple monthly summary you can actually read — and use to make better decisions.
1
Role & Goal
Tell AI you need help understanding your finances — not just calculating numbers, but explaining what they mean for your business.
2
Steps
Ask AI to organize your income and expenses into categories, calculate key totals, flag anything unusual, and write a plain-language summary.
3
Output
Ask for a short summary in plain English — not a spreadsheet — plus 2–3 specific things to pay attention to next month.
4
Reflection
Ask AI to confirm that every number matches what you gave it, and that the advice is practical for a small independent business.
Example prompt
You are a small business financial advisor helping the owner of a Dominican hair salon in the Bronx.

My goal is to understand where my money is going each month and whether I am making a profit.

Here is my income and spending for last month:
[paste your numbers here]

Step 1: Sort my income and expenses into clear categories — rent, supplies, staff, marketing, etc.
Step 2: Calculate my total income, total expenses, and net profit or loss.
Step 3: Flag any category where I spent more than 30% of my income.
Step 4: Write a 3-sentence plain-English summary of what these numbers mean for my business.

Give me the summary first, then the full breakdown underneath. Use simple language — no accounting jargon.

Before you finish: check that all the numbers I gave you are included and that the totals add up correctly.
Fill-in-the-blank template
You are a small business financial advisor helping the owner of [your type of business] in [your city or neighborhood].

My goal is to understand [where my money is going / whether I am making a profit / why my cash feels tight].

Here is my income and spending for [last month / this quarter / this year]:
[paste your numbers here — POS sales records, receipts, invoices, or any record of your financial transactions]

Step 1: Sort my income and expenses into clear categories.
Step 2: Calculate my total income, total expenses, and net profit or loss.
Step 3: Flag any category where I spent more than [20% / 30%] of my income.
Step 4: Write a [3 / 5]-sentence plain-English summary of what these numbers mean.

Give me the summary first, then the full breakdown underneath. No accounting jargon.

Before you finish: confirm all my numbers are included and the totals add up correctly.
💡
What to attach or paste in
You can paste in your POS sales records, a summary from your accounting software, copies of your invoices and receipts, or any other record of your financial transactions — AI does not need a perfectly formatted spreadsheet to work with. The more complete your numbers, the more useful the summary will be. Never share passwords, full account numbers, or Social Security numbers with any AI tool.
3
Finding Time for Everything
Use AI to draft the routine emails, messages, and replies that eat up your week — so you can spend that time running your business.
1
Role & Goal
Tell AI what kind of message you need and who you are writing it to — a customer, supplier, landlord, or team member.
2
Steps
Ask AI to draft the message in your voice, keep it short, put the main point in the first two sentences, and write a subject line if it is an email.
3
Output
Ask for a draft ready to send with minor edits — not a template you have to rewrite. Specify tone: professional, friendly, firm.
4
Reflection
Ask AI to re-read the message and check that the tone matches the relationship, the request is clear, and nothing sounds rude or confusing.
Example prompt
You are helping the owner of a small catering business in Brooklyn write a professional message.

I need to follow up with a client who hired us three weeks ago and still hasn't paid their invoice.

Step 1: Write a short, firm but polite email reminding them that payment is overdue.
Step 2: Mention the invoice number (INV-2025-047) and the amount owed (,200).
Step 3: Give them two ways to pay: Zelle or check. Include a deadline of 7 days.
Step 4: Write a subject line that is clear and professional.

Keep the email under 100 words. Tone: businesslike — not angry, not apologetic.

Before you finish: read it as the client would. Does it feel respectful but firm? If it sounds too aggressive or too soft, adjust it.
Fill-in-the-blank template
You are helping the owner of [your type of business] in [your city] write a professional message.

I need to [follow up on a late payment / respond to a complaint / confirm an order / send a quote / introduce my business].

Step 1: Write a short [email / text message / letter] that [describes what you need to say].
Step 2: Include these details: [names, amounts, dates, or reference numbers].
Step 3: [Add a deadline / Include contact details / Give two options for next steps].
Step 4: Write a clear professional subject line (for emails).

Keep it under [100 / 150] words. Tone: [friendly / professional / firm but polite / warm].

Before you finish: read it as the person receiving it. Is the main point clear in the first two sentences? Does the tone match the relationship? If not, revise.
💡
Save time every week with this
Write prompts once for the messages you send most often — late payment reminders, booking confirmations, supplier requests, complaint responses. Save them in a notes app. Next time, paste in your saved prompt, update the specific details, and you are done in under two minutes.

Researching Better Options
Use AI to research suppliers, compare pricing strategies, or prepare for a negotiation — so costs don't catch you off guard.
1
Role & Goal
Tell AI you are trying to start taking online appointments so clients can book without calling you.
2
Steps
Ask AI to name and explain your options in plain language, compare them on cost and ease of use, and recommend the most realistic one for a small business.
3
Output
Ask for a comparison in simple terms — not technical language — a clear recommendation with a reason, and a first step you can take today.
4
Reflection
Ask AI to confirm every tool it recommends has a free tier or is affordable for a small business, and that setup does not require a developer.
Example prompt
You are a technology advisor helping the owner of a small nail salon in Sunset Park, Brooklyn.

My goal is to start taking online appointments so clients can book without calling me.

Step 1: Name 3 booking apps that work well for a small salon. Explain each in one or two plain sentences — no tech jargon.
Step 2: Compare them on: monthly cost, ease of setup, and whether they send automatic reminders to clients.
Step 3: Recommend the one easiest to get started with for someone not very comfortable with technology.
Step 4: Give me 5 simple steps to set it up for the first time.

Write this as if explaining to someone good at running a business but who has never set up software before.

Before you finish: confirm your top recommendation has a free plan or costs less than 0/month, and that I can set it up on my own without hiring anyone.
Fill-in-the-blank template
You are a technology advisor helping the owner of [your type of business] in [your city or neighborhood].

My goal is to [start taking online appointments / accept payments on my phone / set up an inventory system / send automated messages to customers].

Step 1: Name [3 / 4] tools or apps that work well for a small [type of business]. Explain each in one or two plain sentences — no tech jargon.
Step 2: Compare them on: [monthly cost / ease of setup / customer support / works on phone / free trial].
Step 3: Recommend the easiest option for someone who is [not very tech-savvy / on a tight budget / running the business alone].
Step 4: Give me [3 / 5] simple steps to set it up for the first time.

Write this as if explaining to someone good at running a business but new to this type of software.

Before you finish: confirm your top recommendation has a free plan or costs less than [0 / 0]/month, and that I can set it up without hiring a developer.
💡
Already using a tool? AI can help with that too
If you already use Square, QuickBooks, Google Business, Shopify, or any other tool but feel like you are only using 20% of what it can do — tell AI the name of the tool and what you are trying to accomplish, and ask it to walk you through the specific feature you need. You do not have to start from scratch.

Digital Advertising — Google & Instagram Ads
Use AI to research, plan, and set up a cost-effective digital ad strategy — without wasting money on ads that don't reach the right people.
1
Role & Goal
Tell AI what kind of business you run, who your ideal customer is, and what you want the ad to make them do — visit, call, buy, book.
2
Steps
Ask AI to compare Google Ads vs Instagram Ads for your business type, recommend which to start with, suggest a realistic budget, and write your first ad.
3
Output
Ask for a plain-language comparison, a recommended starting budget, a first ad draft you can use immediately, and one clear next step to launch.
4
Reflection
Ask AI to confirm the recommended budget is realistic for a small business just starting out, and that the ad copy speaks directly to your target customer.
Example prompt
You are a digital marketing advisor helping the owner of a small plumbing business in Jackson Heights, Queens.

My goal is to get more local homeowners to call me when they need a plumber. I have never run paid ads before and my budget is around 00–00/month.

Step 1: Explain in plain language whether Google Ads or Instagram Ads would work better for a local plumbing business, and why.
Step 2: Suggest a realistic monthly budget split between the two platforms, or recommend just one if that makes more sense.
Step 3: Write one Google search ad (headline + 2 description lines) and one Instagram ad caption I can use to start.
Step 4: Give me 3 simple steps to set up my first Google Ads campaign today.

Use plain language throughout — I am not a marketer.

Before you finish: check that the budget you recommend is realistic for a small local business with 00–00/month, and that the ad copy focuses on what a homeowner actually needs to hear before they call a plumber.
Fill-in-the-blank template
You are a digital marketing advisor helping the owner of [your type of business] in [your city or neighborhood].

My goal is to get more [local customers / online shoppers / people in my area] to [call me / visit my shop / book online / buy from my website]. My monthly ad budget is around [00–00 / 00–00 / 00+].

Step 1: Explain in plain language whether Google Ads or Instagram Ads would work better for a [type of business] trying to reach [describe your customer], and why.
Step 2: Suggest a realistic monthly budget split, or recommend just one platform if that makes more sense for my situation.
Step 3: Write one Google search ad (headline + 2 description lines) and one Instagram ad caption I can use to start.
Step 4: Give me [3 / 5] simple steps to set up my first [Google / Instagram] ad campaign today.

Use plain language throughout — I am not a marketer.

Before you finish: confirm the budget you recommend is realistic for a small independently owned business just starting with paid ads, and that the ad copy speaks directly to what my customer actually cares about.
💡
Important: AI plans, you verify before you spend
AI can help you understand your options, write your first ad, and plan a budget — but ad platforms change their interfaces and pricing often. Before you put any money in, verify the setup steps directly on Google Ads (ads.google.com) or Meta Ads Manager (facebook.com/business). Start with a small test budget — 0 or less — before scaling up. Never spend more than you can afford to lose while you are still learning.
